Big Bend

  • Big Bend was made a national park on June 20, 1935.
  • The lowest elevation found in Big Bend is 1,715 feet at the Rio Grande River.
  • The highest elevation found in Big Bend is 7,832 feet on Emory Peak.
  • The name Big Bend comes from a large bend in the Rio Grande River along the park boundary.

Grand Canyon National Park

  • The Grand Canyon creates its own weather.
  • No one actually knows how old it is.
  • It's not the deepest canyon in the world... or even close.
  • There are no dinosaur bones in the Grand Canyon.

Zion National Park

  • Before becoming a National Park, Zion was a national monument.
  • There are plenty of preserved archeological sites in Zion.
  • Zion Canyon is continually changing.
  • Zion has one of the largest freestanding arches in the world.
